TITLE	Drift-diffusion model of hole migration in diamond crystals via states of valence and acceptor bands
ACRONYMS	
KEYWORDS	 boron-doped diamond; insulating side of the Mott transition; transition from valence-band to acceptor-band migration of holes; Nernst-Einstein-Smoluchowski relation; thermal activation_energy of hopping conductivity
ABSTRACT	Ionization equilibrium and dc electrical conductivity of crystalline diamond are considered, for the temperature T j in the vicinity of which valence band (v-band) conductivity is approximately equal to hopping conductivity via acceptors.
ABSTRACT	For the first time, we find explicitly (in the form of definite integrals) the fundamental ratio of diffusion coefficient to drift mobility for both v-band holes and holes hopping via hydrogen-like acceptors for the temperature T j .
ABSTRACT	The known ratios follow from the obtained ones as particular cases.
ABSTRACT	The densities of the spatial distributions of acceptors and hydrogen-like donors as well as of holes are considered to be Poissonian and the fluctuations of electrostatic potential energy are considered to be Gaussian.
ABSTRACT	The dependence of exchange energy of v-band holes on temperature is taken into account.
ABSTRACT	The thermal activation_energy of hopping conduction as a function of the concentration of boron atoms (as acceptors) is calculated for temperature T 3 ≈ T j / 2 .
ABSTRACT	Without the use of any adjustable parameters, the results of calculations quantitatively agree with data obtained from the measurements of hopping conductivity of diamond with boron concentration from 3 × 10 17 to 3 × 10 20 cm-3, i.e. on the insulating side of the Mott phase transition.
__SECTION__ Introduction.
The ionization equilibrium, e.g. between v -band (valence band) holes and acceptor impurity atoms in p -type semiconductor crystals, and the coexistence of v -band and hopping via acceptors migration of holes are considered separately (see, e.g., reviews [ 1 - 3 ]).
However, at such a temperature \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj , when the dc v -band electrical conduction is approximately equal to the dc hopping electrical conduction via hydrogen-like acceptors it is necessary to consider jointly the ionization equilibrium and the drift-diffusion migration of holes.
It is important for the wide bandgap semiconductors, like boron-doped crystalline diamond, for which \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj is in the region from liquid nitrogen to room temperatures and higher.
For the narrow bandgap semiconductors, like boron-doped crystalline silicon, temperature \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj is usually in the region from liquid helium to liquid hydrogen temperatures [ 4 ].
Such a difference in temperatures \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj between boron-doped diamond and boron-doped silicon is connected with that the thermal energy of ionization by electrically neutral boron atoms in diamond is eight times greater than the one in silicon.
Let us note that the wide bandgap semiconductors (for which the energy of electron affinity is less than the band gap) are important for applications in high-temperature electronics and power optics [ 5 ].
In particular, heavily boron-doped diamonds are attractive for studies of superconductivity at liquid helium temperature and higher (see, e.g., [ 6 , 7 ]) and application as electrodes for detection of neurochemicals in the human brain at room temperature [ 8 ].
The purpose of this article is to calculate theoretically (in the framework of the drift-diffusion model) the ratio of the diffusion coefficient to the drift mobility for both v -band holes and acceptor band holes in boron-doped diamond crystals of p -type ( p -Dia:B).
In contrast to other works we consider the temperature region where the contributions of free v -band holes and holes hopping via acceptor band are comparable.
Let us consider a bulk homogeneous diamond crystal that contains v -band holes with the average concentration p , a majority impurity-hydrogen-like acceptors (boron atoms) in the charge states \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (0)? > < /tex-math > \)(0) and \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (-1)? > < /tex-math > \)(-1) with the total average concentration N , and a minority impurity-hydrogen-like donors, all in the charge state \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (+1)? > < /tex-math > \)(+1) , with the average concentration KN , where \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA 0\lt K\lt 1? > < /tex-math > \)0≪K≪1 is the compensation ratio of acceptors by donors.
The electrical neutrality condition at ionization equilibrium for a random distribution of impurity atoms and holes over the crystal volume is < tex-math > < ?CDATA \begin{eqnarray}p+{KN}={N}_{-1},\end{eqnarray}? > < /tex-math > <mml:math> <mml:mi>p</mml:mi> <mml:mo>+</mml:mo> <mml:mi mathvariant="italic">KN</mml:mi> <mml:mo>=</mml:mo> <mml:msub> <mml:mrow> <mml:mi>N</mml:mi> </mml:mrow> <mml:mrow> <mml:mo>-</mml:mo> <mml:mn>1</mml:mn> </mml:mrow> </mml:msub> <mml:mo>,</mml:mo> </math> where \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {N}_{-1}=N-{N}_{0}? > < /tex-math > \)N-1=N-N0 is the average concentration of ionized acceptors, i.e. acceptors in the charge state \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (-1)? > < /tex-math > \)(-1) , N is the average concentration of electrically neutral acceptors.
Holes in the v -band with a total energy E above the threshold of drift-diffusion migration \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA \delta {E}_{v}\lt 0? > < /tex-math > \)δEv≪0 (see figure 1 ) are delocalized over the entire volume of the crystal (between scattering or recombination events) holes in the v -band.
These completely free v -band holes characterize the dc electrical conductivity \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\sigma }_{{\rm{p}}}? > < /tex-math > \)Σp and the stationary Hall effect.
We will denote their concentration as \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {p}_{\mathrm{mob}}? > < /tex-math > \)pmob .
On the other hand, holes in the v -band with energy \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {E}_{{\rm{p}}}\lt \delta {E}_{v}? > < /tex-math > \)Ep≪δEv can only move within restricted regions of a crystal and are probably responsible for the increase of the macroscopic permittivity in doped semiconductors (see, e.g., [ 1 , 9 - 11 ]).
Let \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {p}_{\mathrm{loc}}? > < /tex-math > \)ploc be the concentration of partially free v -band holes.
The total concentration of v -band holes is \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA p={p}_{\mathrm{mob}}+{p}_{\mathrm{loc}}? > < /tex-math > \)p=pmob+ploc .
We will show that \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {p}_{\mathrm{loc}}/p\ll 1? > < /tex-math > \)ploc/p≪1 on the insulating side of the concentration transition insulator-metal (Mott transition) for \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA K\leqslant 0.5? > < /tex-math > \)K≤0.5 in the limit of zero temperature.
In boron-doped moderately compensated ( \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA K\lt 0.1? > < /tex-math > \)K≪0.1 ) diamond of p -type the critical concentration of boron atoms corresponding to the Mott transition is \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA N={N}_{{\rm{M}}}\approx 4\,\times {10}^{20}? > < /tex-math > \)N=NM≈4×1020 cm <sup>-3</sup> [ 12 ].
Impurity atoms are assumed to be localized at sites of diamond crystal lattice.
However, because holes can transfer from acceptors into the v -band and back, as well as hop between acceptors, the charge states of immobile acceptors \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (0)? > < /tex-math > \)(0) and \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (-1)? > < /tex-math > \)(-1) migrate within crystal [ 13 ].
Conversely, all donors permanently remain in the charge state \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (+1)? > < /tex-math > \)(+1) , so their charge states do not migrate.
At temperatures \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA T\lt {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)T≪Tj the electrical conductivity is mainly determined by holes hopping from acceptors in the charge state \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (0)? > < /tex-math > \)(0) to acceptors in the charge state \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA (-1)? > < /tex-math > \)(-1) : the HC regime in figure 1 .
Turbulent (jumping, relay) migration of holes is realized in the vicinity of the temperature \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj , when the v -band \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\sigma }_{{\rm{p}}}? > < /tex-math > \)Σp and the hopping \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\sigma }_{{\rm{h}}}? > < /tex-math > \)Σh via acceptors electrical conductivities are approximately equal to each other (JC regime).
At temperatures \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA T\gt {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)T≫Tj the electrical conductivity is mainly determined by v -band holes, that move 'freely' in the crystal matrix between events of scattering on phonons and impurity atoms (BC regime).
The expression for the density of the stationary (direct) electric current of mobile ions and conduction electrons in solids taking into account drift and diffusion components of the current was first written by Wagner [ 14 ]; see also [ 15 ].

__SECTION__ Conclusions.
The ionization equilibrium and the migration of holes at temperatures close to the temperature \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Tj , for which the dc electrical conductivity of the v -band holes is approximately equal to the dc hopping electrical conductivity of holes via boron atoms (hydrogen-like acceptors), are studied for a p -type diamond crystal.
For the first time, the ratios of the diffusion coefficient to the drift mobility are calculated for both v -band holes ( \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\xi }_{{\rm{p}}}={{eD}}_{{\rm{p}}}/{\mu }_{{\rm{p}}}{k}_{{\rm{B}}}{T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Ξp=eDp/µpkBTj ) and holes hopping via boron atoms ( \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\xi }_{{\rm{h}}}={{eD}}_{{\rm{h}}}/{M}_{{\rm{h}}}{k}_{{\rm{B}}}{T}_{{j}}? > < /tex-math > \)Ξh=eDh/MhkBTj ).
A quantitative estimate for the shift \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA \delta {E}_{{v}}? > < /tex-math > \)δEv of the valence band into the band gap as a function of the boron atom (majority impurity) concentration and the compensation ratio is also given.
The dependence of the thermal activation_energy \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\varepsilon }_{3}? > < /tex-math > \)ε3 of the dc hopping electrical conductivity of p -Dia:B on the concentration of boron atoms at the temperature \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {T}_{3}={T}_{{j}}/2? > < /tex-math > \)T3=Tj/2 and moderate compensation ratios ( K = 0.1-0.5) of acceptors by hydrogen-like donors is calculated.
For diamond with boron concentration N in the range from \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA 3\times {10}^{17}? > < /tex-math > \)3×1017 to \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA 3\times {10}^{20}? > < /tex-math > \)3×1020 cm <sup>-3</sup> (i.e. on the insulating side of the Mott transition) we find quantitative agreement of calculation results with the experimental values of \( < tex-math > < ?CDATA {\varepsilon }_{3}? > < /tex-math > \)ε3 obtained from measurements of the thermal dependence of hopping electrical conductivity.
